## ENDOCRINE — RAAS & ALDOSTERONE *(State scope)*

### Quick Reference: RAAS Cascade
```
Low BP / Low Na⁺ / Sympathetic activation
        ↓
JG cells → RENIN
        ↓
Angiotensinogen (liver) → Angiotensin I → ACE (lung) → Angiotensin II
        ↓
① Adrenal zona glomerulosa → ALDOSTERONE → Na⁺/H₂O retention → ↑BP
② Vasoconstriction → ↑BP
③ Hypothalamus → ↑ADH → water retention
④ Thirst
```

**[State]** Which cells secrete renin, and what stimulates them?
- A. Juxtaglomerular (JG) cells; stimulated by low blood pressure, low Na⁺ delivery to the macula densa, and sympathetic (β1) activation ✓

**[State]** ACE is found predominantly in the: **Lung** *(pulmonary endothelium)*

**[State]** Aldosterone's mechanism of action in the collecting duct:
- B. Binds intracellular mineralocorticoid receptors → increases transcription of ENaC and Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase → increased Na⁺ reabsorption and K⁺ excretion ✓

**[State]** A patient with primary hyperaldosteronism (Conn's syndrome). Expected lab findings:
- B. Low renin, high aldosterone, hypokalemia, metabolic alkalosis ✓

**[State]** Why does primary hyperaldosteronism cause low renin?
- B. Elevated blood pressure and Na⁺/volume expansion from excess aldosterone suppresses renin via negative feedback ✓

**[State]** ADH's mechanism in the collecting duct:
- B. Inserts aquaporin-2 (AQP2) channels into the apical membrane, increasing water reabsorption ✓

**[State]** In SIADH, expected combination:
- C. Low serum Na⁺, high urine osmolality ✓

**[State — True/False]**
- Renin is produced by juxtaglomerular cells of the afferent arteriole. **TRUE**
- ACE is found primarily in the liver. **FALSE** *(primarily in the lung)*
- Aldosterone acts on nuclear receptors to increase transcription of ENaC. **TRUE**
- Primary hyperaldosteronism is associated with high renin and high aldosterone. **FALSE** *(low renin, high aldosterone)*

**[State+]** Trace the complete RAAS response triggered by hemorrhagic shock. Which sequence is correct?
- B. Low BP → JG cells → renin → angiotensinogen (liver) → angiotensin I → ACE (lung) → angiotensin II → vasoconstriction + aldosterone (adrenal zona glomerulosa) + ADH (hypothalamus/posterior pituitary) → Na⁺/H₂O retention + vasoconstriction → restored BP and volume ✓
